# Strategic Counsel Output Quality Checklist

**Checklist ID:** ADVISORY-CL-001
**Referenced by:** tasks/review.md
**Purpose:** Validate strategic counsel deliverables for quality before delivery to user.

[[LLM: INITIALIZATION INSTRUCTIONS

This checklist validates strategic counsel output specifically.

EXECUTION APPROACH:
1. For each category, verify every item against the deliverable
2. Mark items as [x] Pass, [ ] Fail, [N/A] Not Applicable
3. CRITICAL items block delivery; non-critical items are advisory

CRITICAL items are marked with (CRITICAL) suffix.]]

---

## 1. Multi-Perspective Analysis

- [ ] Multiple perspectives or scenarios are considered, not just one path (CRITICAL)
- [ ] Dissenting viewpoints are represented — not just confirmation bias
- [ ] Second-order effects are explored (what happens after the first move?)
- [ ] Historical precedents or analogies are referenced where relevant
- [ ] Stakeholder impact is assessed across affected parties

## 2. Risk Identification

- [ ] Key risks are explicitly identified and categorized (CRITICAL)
- [ ] Probability and impact of each risk are estimated
- [ ] Mitigation strategies or contingency plans are proposed
- [ ] Worst-case scenario is addressed honestly, not minimized
- [ ] Irreversibility of decisions is flagged where applicable

## 3. Assumptions & Evidence

- [ ] Assumptions underlying the recommendation are stated explicitly (CRITICAL)
- [ ] Evidence or data supports the analysis, not just opinion
- [ ] Information gaps are acknowledged, not glossed over
- [ ] Sources of uncertainty are identified
- [ ] Confidence level in the recommendation is stated

## 4. Actionable Recommendations

- [ ] Recommendations are specific and actionable — not vague platitudes (CRITICAL)
- [ ] Clear next steps are defined with owners and timelines
- [ ] Decision criteria are provided: how will the user know if this is working?
- [ ] Go/no-go triggers or milestones are identified
- [ ] Resource requirements are estimated (time, money, people)

## 5. Strategic Coherence

- [ ] Recommendations align with stated goals and constraints
- [ ] Trade-offs are made explicit — what are you giving up?
- [ ] Short-term vs long-term implications are distinguished
- [ ] Competitive dynamics are considered
- [ ] Advice is calibrated to the user's stage and resources

## 6. Communication Quality

- [ ] Analysis is structured logically and easy to follow
- [ ] Executive summary captures the essence in 3-5 sentences
- [ ] Jargon is minimized or defined
- [ ] Conclusion is definitive — not "it depends" without resolution

---

## PASS/FAIL Criteria

**PASS:** All CRITICAL items [x] and fewer than 3 non-critical failures.
**REVISE:** All CRITICAL items [x] but 3+ non-critical failures.
**FAIL:** Any CRITICAL item unchecked.
